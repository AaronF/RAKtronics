Setup in early 2013 RAKtronics is mostly me, Richard Fisher.

With over 30 years in the business I class myself as an experienced Design Engineer in Electronics Product Development. During this time I have worked in a number of sectors and many of my designs and products are in daily use all over the globe.

I am experienced in developing products from the initial concept, through the design and prototype stages and then working with subcontractors to preparing for volume production.

As well as the electronic design I also have extensive software skills both in embedded firmware and PC/Mac desktop applications. This, coupled with a knowledge of mechanical, electrical and pneumatic design, means I can handle most aspects of a whole project.

Based in Northamptonshire in the UK we can develop your project right from the concept stage through to getting ready for the manufacturing process.

Software

Embedded

As a result of my electronics work I started embedded development some time ago. Since then I have done many projects from a simple bedside lamp controller to a multi-channel footfall counting system and data logging system. The bedside lamp controller has been operating my bedside lamp for the last 20 or so years, the footfall counting system has been successfully deployed in many thousands of retail sites throughout the world, is in daily use and is providing data critical to the operation of these stores. Most of my own systems are based around the 8 and 16 bit devices from Microchip but have written for other platforms. I mainly code in C and drop in to assembler when necessary (not very often).

PC/Mac development

This again falls into two categories. Full blown applications These are full applications that generally have an easy to operate user interface and are written to be installed and used by untrained users. Examples of this is the Veripet software that inspects up to 16 bottles per second and can be programmed for many different bottle types. Test and configuration software These are generally more simple applications that are targeted towards a single task and to be used by more experienced people. An application may be designed to interface with a piece of external electronics or device and to be able to configure any settings on that device and then test the device by sending and receiving a series of commands. I mainly code in VB6, vb.net, some C#, and Xojo (mac). Interfacing to other pieces of equipment is not a problem I have done internal devices (ADC, DAC, DIO, etc), USB, Zigbee, Serial and Ethernet in the past. Some applications use multithreading for efficient operation.

Mobile apps

A new area!
Available on Android and iOS, we recently developed out first app for the getWaiter! project. It is a relatively simple app but does involve QR code reading and securely interacting with a cloud based server. Presently we use phonegap for the apps but will be looking into native apps in the future.
